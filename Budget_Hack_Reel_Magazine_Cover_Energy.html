Design Principles: Magazine Cover Energy

What makes this design language distinct from the Desi Scrapbook approach

Typography Is the Design

No stickers, no doodles, no emojis. The Playfair Display headline at the right size, weight, and position IS the visual. Every frame could work in black and white â€” the typography carries it alone.

White Space = Confidence

Resist the urge to fill every pixel. The empty space between elements tells the viewer: "we're so good, we don't need to shout." Every magazine cover has generous margins â€” your reel frames should too.

Two-Color Maximum Per Frame

Each frame uses at most two dominant colors. Burgundy + gold on dark. Burgundy + off-white on cream. This restraint is what creates the "premium" feeling. More colors = more chaos = less authority.

Editorial Structure

Header bars, footer rules, page numbers, section labels â€” these aren't decorative. They signal "this is a publication, not a random post." It positions Swan Stories as a media brand from day one.

Data Without Decoration

Budget breakdowns use clean bar charts and dotted-line tables â€” like financial journalism. No pie charts, no 3D effects. The numbers themselves are visually striking because of size and typography, not because of flashy graphics.

Pull-Quote Treatment

When you include a couple's quote, frame it like a magazine pull-quote: gold left-border, italic Cormorant Garamond, attribution with city. This elevates a simple testimonial into editorial content.

Consistent Frame Architecture

Every light frame has: header bar (label + page number) â†’ content â†’ footer bar (brand + page number). This creates rhythm and recognition across slides. The viewer subconsciously registers "this is a series."
